Chicken-Karahi

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 30 mins
  • Cook Time: 35 mins
  • Total Time: 1 hr 5 mins
  • Servings: 6

descripsion

This dish gets its name from a karahi, a flat open pot used a lot in Pakistani and Indian cooking. In spite of its name, this recipe can be made in any pot. it's a little spicy (by Pakistani standards), but simple and foolproof.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup sunflower oil
  • 1 whole chicken, giblets discarded, cut these into 12 pieces
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 1/2 ginger and garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoons red chile powder
  • 1/4 teaspoons ground turmeric
  • 6 ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 4 green chile peppers, finely chopped
  • 1 bunch fresh cilanto leaves, finely chopped

Directions

Step 1: Initial Fry & Spice Infusion

Begin by heating oil in a large pot or karahi on high flame. Add the chicken pieces and sauté for around 5 minutes until they begin to brown. Pour in some water, then mix in cumin, salt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, and turmeric. Stir everything together and let it cook for about 30 seconds until the aroma is released.

Step 2: Simmer With Tomatoes & Chillies

Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies to the pot, stirring them in. Lower the heat to a gentle simmer, cover with a lid, and cook for about 30 minutes—until the chicken is fully cooked through and no longer pink at the bone. For accuracy, the internal temperature near the bone should reach 165°F (74°C).

Step 3: Garnish with Cilantro

Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and let them cook briefly—about 30 seconds—just until they start to wilt.
